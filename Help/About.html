What is a CanSat

A can-sized satellite is a crucial fundamental practical experience, which creates an opportunity to explore aerospace as one of the challenging technologies. The can-sat as it is shortly written, was firstly introduced by professor Bob Twiggs in November 1998 at the university space systems symposium (USSS) for the purpose of providing an affordable way to acquire the researchers with the basic knowledge to many challenges in building a satellite.

What is a CanSat Ground Station

Ground stations are surface-based facilities which are designed to provide real-time communication with satellites. The crew at these stations send radio signals to the satellite (uplink), receive data transmissions from the satellite (downlink), and in some cases,serve as command and control centers for the satellite network. From the ground station, data can be analyzed (or relayed to another location for analysis), the altitude and movement of the satellite and information about its critical systems can be monitored, and satellites can be controlled by decision makers.
